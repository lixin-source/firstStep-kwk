While college tuition is expensive, there are many scholarships to reduce tuition costs. Below, firstStep provides a few applications for a scholarship.


Courage to Grow

Courage to Grow awards one student a $500 scholarship each month with the mission to ease the financial burden of a college education. The application is a simple 250-word essay!

Eligibility Requirements:

  • Junior or senior in high school or college student with minimum GPA of 2.5
  • U.S. citizenship
  • Deadline: August 31

    • We Belong in College

    We Belong in College aims to encourage students to pursue a college education by offerring scholarships in exchange for personal accounts of times when students questioned whether they belonged in college. They found that when students share stories with others, both parties are uplifted!

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Attendance in or attending a college in the U.S.
  • Residency in the U.S.
  • Deadline: August 31

    • The Dream.US

    The Dream.US provides scholarships to highly-qualified DREAMers and undocumented students who qualify for DACA to help them pay for their undergraduate degree. It pays for the tuition if you enroll in a partner college with the Dream.US.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Senior in high school or community college graduates
  • Have current DACA or TPS status or meet their immigration eligibility criteria
  • Not Open Yet
  • Deadline: February 27

    • Fontana Transport Inc. Scholars Program

    First-generation, low-income, and undocumented students are all welcome to apply to this scholarship up to $5,000 in value. Fontana Transport Inc. is a family-owned company looking to help individuals who are looking towards higher education as a way to help themselves, their families, and their community.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Attending a college in the U.S.
  • Minimum weighted 3.5 high school GPA
  • Must graduate high school in Spring or Summer
  • Plans to study in Transportation Management, Math, Sceince, Engineering, Architecture, Environmental Deisng, Pre-med, Psychology, or Spanish Language/Literature
  • Deadline: April 19

    • Golden Doors Scholars

    The Golden Doors scholarship provides funding to highly-qualified DREAMers and undocumented students who qualify for DACA to help them pay for their undergraduate degree. Its partner colleges include Smith College, Duke, and more.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Senior in high school, high school graduates, and transfer students in the U.S.
  • Have current DACA or TPS status or meet their immigration eligibility criteria
  • Not Open Yet
  • Deadline: N/A

    • Coca Cola Scholars Foundation

    The Golden Doors scholarship provides funding to highly-qualified DREAMers and undocumented students who qualify for DACA to help them pay for their undergraduate degree. Its partner colleges include Smith College, Duke, and more.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • U.S. high school student graduating in the 2020-2021 academic school year
  • U.S. Citizen, U.S. National, U.S. Permanent Resident, Refugees, Asylees, Cuban-Haitian Entrants, or Humanitarian Parolees based on the guidelines HERE.
  • Planning to attend college
  • Deadline: October 31 @5PM EST

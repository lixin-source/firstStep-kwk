While college tuition is expensive, there are many scholarships to reduce tuition costs. Below, firstStep provides a few applications for a scholarship.


Hispanic Scholarship Fund

The HSF Scholarship assists students of Hispanic heritage obtain a university degree. Scholarships are available, on a competitive basis, to high school seniors, undergraduate students (all years), community college students transferring to four year universities, and graduate students.

Eligibility Requirements:

  • Must be of Hispanic heritage
  • Minimum of 3.0 GPA on a 4.0 scale (or equivalent) for high school students
  • Minimum of 2.5 GPA on a 4.0 scale (or equivalent) for undergraduate and graduate students
  • Plan to enroll full-time in an accredited, not-for-profit, four-year university, or graduate school, in the US, for the 2020-2021 academic year
  • US Citizen, Permanent Legal Resident, DACA or Eligible Non-Citizen (as defined by FAFSA)
  • Complete FAFSA or state based financial aid application (if applicable)
  • Amount: Merit & need based, $500 to $5,000

    • Opens: January 1, 2021

    Deadline: February 15, 2021


    InternMakers Scholarship

    The InternMakers scholarship selects students in high school and/or university with finances and mentorship with its co-founders.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Attendance in or attending a college in the U.S.
  • Residency in the U.S.
  • Amount: 1st Place = $1000, 2nd Place = $500

    • Deadline: August 31


    The Dream.US

    The Dream.US provides scholarships to highly-qualified DREAMers and undocumented students who qualify for DACA to help them pay for their undergraduate degree. It pays for the tuition if you enroll in a partner college with the Dream.US.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Senior in high school or community college graduates
  • Have current DACA or TPS status or meet their immigration eligibility criteria
  • Amount: $33,000

    • Opens: November 1, 2020

    Deadline: February 27, 2021


    Fontana Transport Inc. Scholars Program

    Fontana Transport Inc. is a family-owned company looking to help individuals who are looking towards higher education as a way to help themselves, their families, and their community.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Attending a college in the U.S.
  • Minimum weighted 3.5 high school GPA
  • Must graduate high school in Spring or Summer
  • Plans to study in Transportation Management, Math, Sceince, Engineering, Architecture, Environmental Deisng, Pre-med, Psychology, or Spanish Language/Literature
  • Amount: Up to $5,000

    • Deadline: April 19, 2021


    Golden Doors Scholars

    The Golden Doors scholarship provides funding to highly-qualified DREAMers and undocumented students who qualify for DACA to help them pay for their undergraduate degree. Its partner colleges include Smith College, Duke, Emory, and more.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Senior in high school, high school graduates, and transfer students in the U.S.
  • Have current DACA or TPS status or meet their immigration eligibility criteria
  • Amount: Not Stated

    • Deadline: October 25, 2020 at 11:59pm EST


    Coca Cola Scholars Foundation

    The Coca-Cola Scholars Program scholarship is an achievement-based scholarship awarded to graduating high school seniors.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Current high school (or home-schooled) student attending a school in the U.S. (or select DoD schools) who will graduate during the 2020-2021 academic school year
  • U.S. Citizens, U.S. Nationals, U.S. Permanent Residents, Refugees, Asylees, Cuban-Haitian Entrants, or Humanitarian Parolees - based on the guidelines utilized by U.S. Department of Education for Federal Financial Aid eligibility
  • Will receive high school diploma during current academic year
  • Planning to pursue a degree at an accredited U.S. post-secondary institution
  • Amount: $20,000

    • Deadline: October 31, 2020 at 5pm EST

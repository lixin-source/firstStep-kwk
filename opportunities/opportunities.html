In order to develop a career plan for yourself, or to see what you are interested in, it is beneficial to experience different fields and talk to professionals. Visit the links below for more opportunities.

The Brown Scholars Program

A 3-year, tuition-free intensive program for high school girls, focused on the intersection of computer science and science.

Eligibility Requirements:

  • 9th and 10th grade high school student
  • Self-identifying woman
  • Deadline: September 3

    • America on Tech Summer Coding Camp

    A week-long immersive program in web design & development. Students will work individually and in teams, building a portfolio of online projects using HTML, CSS, and other industry-aligned tools such as Bootstrap and Repl.it. The Bootcamp is comprised of 2 sessions a day for 5 days, and features 6 session challenges and 1 virtual team competition.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Current sophomore or junior high school student
  • A high school student from a New York City public or charter school
  • Identify as an underrepresented demographic in technology
  • Deadline: September 4

    • UndocuProfessionals Virtual Conference

    Virtual conference offerred for undocumented students and allies to learn about career development for DACA and non-DACA individuals.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Open to the community
  • Date: September 14

    • InsideSherpa Virtual Internships

    A site that includes virtual internships in subjects like technology, business, etc. from companies like Microsoft, JP Morgan Chase, and more.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Open to the community
  • Deadline: Ongoing

    • Helyx Research Program

    For under-represented minorities, low income, and POC youth interested in scientific research. Applicants who are accepted are paired with a mentor and work in a group to develop projects. Applicants do not need prior research experience, and it is free.

    Eligibility Requirements:

  • Must be a high school student
  • Deadline: September 10
